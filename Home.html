The Battle of Stalingrad was one of if not the most bloody battle in human history. Fought from the 23rd of August 1942 until the 2nd of February 1942 this Battle was a key turning point on the Eastern front and marked the decline of the Wehrmacht. The crushing defeat at this bombed out wasteland of concrete and steel would halt the Axis advance and turn the tide against them. The intention behind the seizure of Stalingrad was mainly to control the area for control of oil fields farther south. These oil fields in the Caucuses were key to the Red Army, they supplied much of the oil needed for their armored divisions and air force. Because of this Hitler was keen to attempt to seize these in order to weaken the Soviets. The Germans had been repelled at Moscow only a few miles from the city and were now unable to capture the city due to Soviet reinforcements even with the help of Hungarian, Croatian, Italian and other allies. The Red Army had modernized and was much better prepared now than it had been in the opening days of Operation Barbarossa which had lost it millions of men. With new T-34 tanks and Yak-9 fighter planes the Soviet forces were much better prepared to counter and repel the invaders. However their ability to use these vehicles would be compromised if the Germans managed to seize their oil fields. To achieve this end the Fourth Panzer Army, Hungarian Second Army, several Italian infantry divisions, the Seventeenth Army, First Panzer Army, and the Sixth Army. By the end of July the Germans had managed to push across the Don River and were only forty miles from the Volga River. Hitler had ordered that the force be split into two groups the second of which, Group B, would be tasked with capturing Stalingrad. This force was mainly made of the 4th Panzer Army and the German 6th Army as well as several Allied Armies.
